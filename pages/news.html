The Age of Consent in Digital Services

A major debate ongoing as of July 2026 revolves around the age of consent in digital services, with many arguing that the current age of 13, as stipulated by COPPA, is too low. Advocates for raising the age of consent point to the need for greater protection of minors' data and the importance of ensuring that young users fully understand the implications of their online activities. Others argue that a higher age of consent could unfairly restrict access to information and educational resources for teenagers.

02

Balancing National Security with Individual Privacy Rights

The balance between national security concerns and the protection of individual privacy rights continues to be a contentious issue. As governments increasingly rely on digital surveillance to combat terrorism and cybercrime, there are growing concerns about the potential for abuse of power and the erosion of civil liberties. This debate is particularly relevant in the context of data privacy, as governments and tech companies navigate the complexities of collecting and analyzing vast amounts of personal data.

03

The Role of Education in Data Privacy

There is an ongoing discussion about the importance of incorporating data privacy education into school curricula. Proponents argue that educating young people about online safety, data protection, and digital rights is crucial for preparing them to navigate the complexities of the digital world safely and responsibly. This includes teaching minors how to manage their online presence, recognize phishing attempts, and understand the terms of service of online platforms.

04

Regulating Big Tech: The Future of Antitrust Law

The future of antitrust law and its application to Big Tech companies is a topic of significant debate. As these companies continue to grow in size and influence, there are concerns about their impact on competition, innovation, and consumer choice. Proposed reforms aim to address issues such as data monopolies, unfair business practices, and the concentration of market power, with the goal of promoting a more competitive and equitable digital marketplace.
