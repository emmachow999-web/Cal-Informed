Should Social Media Companies Be Required to Verify Users' Ages?

There is an ongoing debate about whether social media companies should be required to verify users' ages to prevent minors from accessing inappropriate content. Proponents argue that age verification is necessary to protect young people from harm, while opponents argue that it would be an invasion of privacy and could lead to censorship. As of March 6, 2026, the debate is ongoing, with some companies already implementing age verification measures and others resisting the idea.

Is the Use of Facial Recognition Technology a Threat to Personal Privacy?

The use of facial recognition technology is becoming increasingly common, but it raises serious concerns about personal privacy. Critics argue that the technology could be used to track individuals without their consent, while proponents argue that it is a useful tool for law enforcement and national security. As the technology continues to evolve, the debate over its use and regulation is likely to continue.

03

Should Teens Have the Right to Delete Their Online History?

There is a growing debate about whether teens should have the right to delete their online history, including social media posts and other digital activity. Proponents argue that this would give young people more control over their online presence and help to prevent cyberbullying and other forms of online harassment. Opponents argue that deleting online history could have unintended consequences, such as erasing important evidence of online activity.

04

Is the Collection of Biometric Data by Tech Companies a Threat to Personal Privacy?

The collection of biometric data, such as fingerprints and facial recognition data, by tech companies is becoming increasingly common. However, this raises serious concerns about personal privacy, as biometric data is highly sensitive and could be used to track individuals without their consent. As the use of biometric data continues to grow, the debate over its regulation and protection is likely to intensify.
