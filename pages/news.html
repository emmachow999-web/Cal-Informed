The Biggest Privacy Questions Right Now

These debates shape the laws of tomorrow — and they affect you directly.

01

The Future of Online Privacy Regulation

As of April 24, 2026, there is an ongoing debate about the future of online privacy regulation, with some arguing that the US should adopt a comprehensive federal privacy law, similar to the EU's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Others argue that a federal law would stifle innovation and that state-level regulations, such as California's Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), are more effective.

02

The Impact of AI on Data Privacy

There is a growing concern about the impact of AI on data privacy, as AI-powered technologies are increasingly used to collect, analyze, and share personal data. Some argue that AI can help improve data privacy by detecting and preventing data breaches, while others argue that AI poses significant risks to data privacy, particularly if it is used to create detailed profiles of individuals.

03

The Role of Tech Companies in Protecting Minors' Online Privacy

There is an ongoing debate about the role of tech companies in protecting minors' online privacy, with some arguing that companies have a responsibility to prioritize minors' safety and well-being online. Others argue that parents and guardians should take primary responsibility for protecting minors' online privacy, and that tech companies should provide tools and resources to support them.

04

The Tension Between National Security and Data Privacy

There is a long-standing debate about the tension between national security and data privacy, with some arguing that governments need access to personal data to prevent terrorism and other security threats. Others argue that such access poses significant risks to data privacy and that there are alternative ways to ensure national security that do not involve mass surveillance.
