California's Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) has been expanded to include new protections for consumers, including the right to opt-out of the sale of their personal data and the right to request that companies delete their data. The Use of AI in Education: Balancing Personalization with Privacy

The impact of social media on teen mental health is a topic of ongoing debate. While some argue that social media can provide a sense of connection and community, others argue that it can exacerbate feelings of loneliness, anxiety, and depression. The Role of Parents in Protecting Teen Online Privacy

As teens spend more time online, there is a growing debate about the role of parents in protecting their online privacy. While some argue that parents should have access to their teens' online activity, others argue that this can be an invasion of their privacy. Parents, educators, and policymakers are grappling with how to balance the need to protect teens from online harm with the need to respect their autonomy and privacy.

This debate is particularly relevant for teens, who are increasingly aware of the importance of protecting their online privacy and security.
