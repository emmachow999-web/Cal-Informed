California's Age-Appropriate Design Code Act Takes Effect

As of April 2026, the Age-Appropriate Design Code Act requires online services to prioritize the well-being and safety of minors. This law, passed in 2022, forces companies like Meta and Google to make significant changes to how they collect and use data from California teenagers. Companies must now conduct data protection impact assessments and provide transparency reports.

As of January 2026, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) now applies to employee data, giving California workers more control over their personal information. Ongoing Debates

The Biggest Privacy Questions Right Now

These debates shape the laws of tomorrow — and they affect you directly.

01

Should Social Media Platforms Be Liable for Harm Caused by User-Generated Content?

As social media platforms face increasing scrutiny over user-generated content, a debate is ongoing about whether these platforms should be held liable for harm caused by such content. Proponents argue that this would incentivize companies to better moderate their platforms, while opponents claim it would stifle free speech and innovation.

02

The Ethics of Biometric Data Collection in Schools

The use of biometric data, such as facial recognition, in schools has sparked a heated debate about student privacy and security. While some argue that biometric data collection can enhance school safety, others claim it poses significant risks to students' privacy and could lead to biased outcomes.

03

The Impact of Data Localization on Global Data Privacy

As countries implement data localization laws, requiring companies to store data within national borders, a debate is emerging about the impact on global data privacy. Some argue that data localization can better protect user data, while others claim it could lead to data fragmentation and undermine global cooperation on data protection.

04

The Tension Between Data Protection and National Security

The balance between data protection and national security is a pressing concern, as governments seek to access user data for security purposes while also protecting citizens' privacy. This debate raises questions about the limits of government surveillance, the role of encryption, and the need for transparency in national security data collection practices.
