01

Should Social Media Companies Be Required To Verify Users' Ages?

As concerns about online safety and data protection continue to grow, there is an ongoing debate about whether social media companies should be required to verify users' ages. Proponents argue that age verification is necessary to protect kids from online harm and to prevent companies from collecting and using their personal data without consent. Opponents argue that age verification is impractical and could infringe on users' right to anonymity. This debate is particularly relevant in light of recent efforts by lawmakers to pass legislation requiring social media companies to verify users' ages.

02

Is The Use Of AI-Powered Surveillance Technology In Schools An Invasion Of Students' Privacy?

The use of AI-powered surveillance technology in schools has sparked a heated debate about students' privacy and the potential risks and benefits of this technology. Proponents argue that surveillance technology can help to improve school safety and prevent incidents of violence. Opponents argue that this technology is an invasion of students' privacy and could be used to discriminate against certain groups of students. As the use of surveillance technology in schools becomes more widespread, this debate is likely to continue.

03

Should Companies Be Allowed To Collect And Use Personal Data From Kids Under 13?

There is an ongoing debate about whether companies should be allowed to collect and use personal data from kids under 13. Proponents argue that companies need to collect data from kids in order to provide them with personalized services and experiences. Opponents argue that kids under 13 are not developmentally ready to make informed decisions about their personal data and that companies should be prohibited from collecting and using their data without parental consent. This debate is particularly relevant in light of recent efforts to update and strengthen laws related to kids' online privacy.

04

Is The Collection And Use Of Biometric Data By Companies A Threat To Consumers' Privacy?

The collection and use of biometric data, such as facial recognition and fingerprint scans, by companies has sparked a debate about the potential risks and benefits of this technology. Proponents argue that biometric data can be used to improve security and convenience for consumers. Opponents argue that the collection and use of biometric data is a threat to consumers' privacy and could be used to discriminate against certain groups of people. As the use of biometric data becomes more widespread, this debate is likely to continue.
